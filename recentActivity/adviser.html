GRADE 12 STEM RESEARCH ADVISER: MISTIFY SYSTEM

SY 2025–2026 - Holy Trinity College
Image Source: https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=1535945961868401&set=pb.100063590364810.-2207520000&type=3

I had the privilege of serving as the Research Adviser for a team of Grade 12 STEM students during School Year 2025–2026. After months of mentoring and guiding the team throughout their research journey, they successfully defended their capstone research on February 23, 2026. Through their outstanding work and innovative engineering solution, the team was officially nominated for the Best Research Award, recognizing the quality and significance of their study.

The team's research, titled "MISTIFY: The Effectiveness of an Arduino Mega-Based System in Detecting and Reducing Indoor Air Pollutants Through Automated Misting," focused on developing an intelligent indoor air quality monitoring and mitigation system. The prototype utilized an Arduino Mega microcontroller integrated with a PMS5003 particulate matter sensor, MH-Z19B carbon dioxide sensor, and DHT22 temperature sensor to continuously monitor environmental conditions. When unsafe air quality levels or temperatures of 35°C or higher were detected, the system automatically activated an ultrasonic misting mechanism, dual DC-powered dispersion fans, and a carbon filtration system to reduce airborne pollutants and improve indoor air quality.

As the research adviser, I mentored the team throughout the research and development process by providing technical guidance on system design, engineering concepts, research methodology, and documentation. I reviewed and refined their manuscript, assisted with data presentation and technical revisions, and worked closely with the students to strengthen the feasibility and implementation of their automation solution. Supporting the team from concept development through the final defense and witnessing their work earn a Best Research Award nomination was both professionally fulfilling and a testament to their dedication and innovation.
